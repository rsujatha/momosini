### [SCREEN: your face, or a title card that says "Momosini"]

Hi, I'm Sujatha, and I am a mom a 11 month old.

I built Momosini —

a concierge agent that plans the day for new parents.

[PAUSE]

Here's the problem that we address.

Conventional planners assumes you control your own schedule.

For a new parent, that's just wrong.

Your day is defined by the time you *don't* control —

nap windows, feeds, childcare handoffs —

and it keeps shifting as your baby grows.

So parents end up feeling like they're failing a plan

that was never realistic to begin with.

[PAUSE]

---

### [SCREEN: quick glimpse of the app — a finished day plan]

So here's what Momosini does.

It builds your day around the time you *don't* control —

your baby's sleep and development —

and reshapes it as your baby grows.

You tell it about your day in plain words,

and it hands back a realistic plan —

with safe play ideas for your baby's stage and nap suggestions.

[PAUSE]

---

### [SCREEN: stay on your face]

So why an agent — and not just a chatbot?

A parent doesn't type tidy calendar entries.

They type things like:

"I'm exhausted, the house is a mess, my mum's here from 10 to 1, I need to polish a paragraph of my proposal, also give me play ideas for my baby."

A chatbot could reply to that.

And instead of a wall of text,

The agent decides when to ask a gentle follow-up

and when it already knows enough to build a structured day plan.

And because it already tracks

the baby's history on this device,

it doesn't start from zero each day —

it learns from the days before,

so each plan grows more tailored

as your baby does.


It does not guess the nap times

and invent the milestones.

Behind the scenes, it decides, on its own,

to call MCP tools

for the baby's real developmental facts,

so nothing is made up.

A chatbot answers.

An agent reasons, uses tools, and acts.

[PAUSE]

---

### [SCREEN: show architecture-diagram.png — hold it here]

Here's how it works.

The parent talks to a web front end.

That calls a single Google ADK agent — the composer.

Crucially, the agent never invents medical facts.

When it needs nap ranges or milestones,

it calls a local MCP server

that reads curated, source-tagged data.

So every developmental number

traces back to a real guideline —

nothing is hallucinated.

Only the play ideas are generated by the model,

and those are bounded by a strict infant-safety rule.

[PAUSE]

### [SCREEN: back to your face]

On privacy:

the backend stores nothing.

The schedule and the baby's birthday

live only in your browser.

The agent only ever sends the baby's age in months —

never names, never details.

[PAUSE]

### [SCREEN: "The Build" card, then a glimpse of code / terminal / Cloud Run]

Now — how it's built.

The agent runs on Google's ADK,

with the day-composition logic

pulled out into an Agent Skill.

The facts live behind a local MCP server

I wrote with FastMCP.

The whole thing —

web app, the agent, and the MCP server —

ships as one Docker container

on Google Cloud Run.

And a pytest harness

fails the build

if the agent ever states a fact

the tools didn't provide.

[PAUSE]
