# Sources & provenance rules

Every figure in every `*.json` here carries a `source` and a `tier`. No bare numbers.

## Tiers
- **[A] consensus guideline** — AASM/AAP, WHO. Least disputable.
- **[B] research literature** — systematic review / longitudinal reference study.
- **[C] practical convention** — widely used, thin primary-literature backing. Flag as such.

## Standing sources
- [A] Paruthi et al., *Recommended Amount of Sleep for Pediatric Populations*, J Clin Sleep
  Med 2016;12(6):785-786 (AAP-endorsed).
- [A] WHO, *Guidelines on physical activity, sedentary behaviour and sleep for under-5s*, 2019.
- [B] Galland et al., *Normal sleep patterns in infants and children: a systematic review*,
  Sleep Med Rev 2012.
- [B] Iglowstein et al., *Sleep Duration From Infancy to Adolescence*, Pediatrics 2003.

Rule: if you can't attach a source and a tier, it doesn't go in the file. See `naps.json`
for the worked example the other domains should match.
